Fiscal Responsibility Measures

The proposed budget incorporates several cost-saving measures to minimize the overall increase:

  1. Level-funding of many operational line items across departments
  2. Reduction of $25,000 in Short-Term Principal debt payments
  3. Elimination of certain expenses including Plowing Private Ways (-$2,100) and Longevity program (-$2,000)
  4. Strategic reductions in supplies and operational expenses in multiple departments
  5. Prioritizing only the most essential personnel adjustments and infrastructure investments

Conclusion

The FY26 Budget Proposal reflects a careful balance between fiscal responsibility and necessary investments in our community's future. The 6.2% increase is primarily driven by education costs and non-discretionary assessments, with municipal operations seeing only modest adjustments.

We believe this budget positions the town to maintain service levels while addressing critical infrastructure and personnel needs. We respectfully request the Board's approval of this fiscally responsible proposal.

Public Safety

FY26 Proposed: $1,581,722

Change from FY25: +$31,175 (2%)

Percentage of Total Budget

13%

Justifications for Major Changes

  1. Police Department (210): The 2% increase ($12,050) reflects moderate adjustments in personnel costs and strategic equipment investments. Key changes include:
    • Standard 3% salary increase for Police Chief
    • Significant investment in Police Equipment (+176% and +55%) to replace aging gear and ensure officer safety
    • Reduction in Police Education (-31%) and Stipends & Allowances (-42%) to offset equipment investments
    • Increase in Services (+1467%) to support new community policing initiatives
  2. Fire Department (220): The 3% decrease (-$16,559) represents careful budget management while maintaining essential services. Key changes include:
    • Standard 3% salary increase for Fire Chief
    • Elimination of Ambulance Lease Space (-$21,365) due to relocation of ambulance services
    • Increased Vehicle Maintenance (+13%) to address aging apparatus maintenance needs
  3. Land Use (241): The 27% increase ($25,270) is primarily driven by staff reorganization and expanded code enforcement activities, offset slightly by reduced supply needs.
  4. Animal Control (292): The 6% increase ($1,177) reflects contractual obligations with the regional animal control program.
  5. Dispatch (299): The 6% increase ($9,238) is a contractual increase for Rutland Regional Dispatch services, which provides essential 911 and emergency communications support.
