The overall Debt Service budget for FY26 remains level-funded at $146,862 compared to FY25, though there are significant shifts within the debt structure:

Justifications for Major Changes

The Liabilities & Assessments budget shows a 5% increase ($50,970) from FY25, representing fixed costs and contractual obligations that are largely beyond the town's direct control:

  1. Worcester Regional Retirement: The 7% increase ($32,823) is a mandated assessment determined by the Worcester Regional Retirement System based on actuarial calculations of the town's pension obligations. This increase reflects the system's funding schedule to address long-term pension liabilities.
  2. Health Insurance: The 8% increase ($16,482) reflects rising healthcare costs nationwide and is based on the rates established by our insurance provider. Despite this increase, our participation in a regional health insurance collaborative has helped moderate premium increases below typical market rates.
  3. Liability Insurance: The 5% increase ($7,809) addresses rising property and casualty insurance costs. This increase has been minimized through our participation in the Massachusetts Interlocal Insurance Association (MIIA) and implementation of risk management practices.
  4. Cherry Sheet Assessments: These state-mandated assessments remain level-funded for FY26, including charges for air pollution control, regional transit, and Registry of Motor Vehicle services.
  5. Medicare: This obligation remains level-funded at $33,360, reflecting stable personnel costs and the town's consistent 1.45% contribution rate for all employees.
  6. Offsets and Overlay: The Library Off-Set remains unchanged, representing state aid that directly supports library operations. The Overlay account, which funds property tax abatements and exemptions, remains level-funded at $60,000.

Fiscal Responsibility Measures

The proposed budget incorporates several cost-saving measures to minimize the overall increase:

  1. Level-funding of many operational line items across departments
  2. Reduction of $25,000 in Short-Term Principal debt payments
  3. Elimination of certain expenses including Plowing Private Ways (-$2,100) and Longevity program (-$2,000)
  4. Strategic reductions in supplies and operational expenses in multiple departments
  5. Prioritizing only the most essential personnel adjustments and infrastructure investments

Conclusion

The FY26 Budget Proposal reflects a careful balance between fiscal responsibility and necessary investments in our community's future. The 6.2% increase is primarily driven by education costs and non-discretionary assessments, with municipal operations seeing only modest adjustments.

We believe this budget positions the town to maintain service levels while addressing critical infrastructure and personnel needs. We respectfully request the Board's approval of this fiscally responsible proposal.
